What have we found by comparing?

    There are many differences between the revolutions. First, they used very different methods. Gandhi's non-violent method is different from the other two. Also, having armed citizens rebel is different from military coups.
    Their goals are different as well. The Bolsheviks wanted to replace the Tsar with a communist government. The Free Officers want to overthrow monarchy. Gandhi wanted India to be independent.
    They all succeeded and influenced other revolutions in the future. Why? This can be answered by their similarities: they all had the courage to take the stand and continue even if there are casualties. For all of the revolutions, the protestors have the courage to stand up against the powerful government. In addition, the casualties didn't soften their determination. They continued the revolution, and reached their final success.
     Therefore, we can conclude that persistence and courage are the most important factor of a successful revolution.
