The Free Officers

“We will fight from house to house, from village to village. We will fight than live humiliated. We are building our country, our history, our future.”39

    The most important event in the Egyptian Revolution of 1952:
    In the July of 1952, the Free Officers movement that were held by two leaders, Mohamed Naguib and Gamal Abdel Nasser.
     King Farouk, who is the dictator at that time, ran away from the palace, but was forced out of Egypt eventually.
     “The order for Farouk to abdicate and depart into exile finally came on Saturday, July 26, 1952 and at 6 o'clock that evening, the king set sail for Italy”.39
     The Free Officers destroyed the palaces and British buildings to show their anger to the British because of their invasion and overthrowing monarchy. Replaced the Prime Minister and many other leading politicians that disagree with this movement even if they had great contribution to the society.4
     After the revolution, Mohamed Naguib became the president and Egypt declared to establish democracy.
